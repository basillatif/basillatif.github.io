Stock Market Data Analysis and Prediction

The stock market is a rich data source that can be used to implement analytics and data science techniques. Every stock has price that changes by the second and associated daily, monthly, and yearly volume with open and close prices.

I have made a great effort to analyze stock prices to figure out predictive signals and long-term trends.

Predicting Long Term Price Trends

In one project at UC Irvine, I worked with a team of 3 other students to find the best predictive algorithm to determine future stock price. For our analysis, we focused on the FAANG technology stocks: Facebook, Apple, Amazon, Netflix, and Google.

3 Algorithms/Tools to Predict Future Price Based on Previous Prices

Stock price is largely determined by investor sentiment and predictions of future gains. Several key economic theories are at play when one tries to predict a stock price. For one, the current price is supposed to reflect future prices. Further, the perceived strength of economy also plays a role in stock prices.

Our analysis looked at stock prices for technology companies over the last 5 years. It is important 2 things. First, the technology sector has done exceptionally well in the past 2 decades and has seen larger gains than the rest of the economy as a whole. Secondly, we are in the midst of a tremendous bull market. Since the recession of 2008, stock prices have risen significantly. Both of these factors impact why our models were able to perform so well.

Monte Carlo Simulation

1. Monte Carlo Simulation - we used the famous Monte Carlo simulation to generate predictions for future prices for Google's stock. According to investopedia, "Monte Carlosimulations are used to model the probability of different outcomes in a process that cannot easily be predicted due to the intervention of random variables. It is a technique used to understand the impact of risk and uncertainty in prediction and forecasting models." The graph below shows that most of the simulations (lines) lie in the middle of the chart and go slightly up. The Monte Carlo simulation for Google's stock price shows a positive trend for 2017.

Facebook Prophet

3. Facebook Prophet was developed by the Data Science Team at Facebook: "Prophet is a procedure for forecasting time series data based on an additive model where non-linear trends are fit with yearly, weekly, and daily seasonality, plus holiday effects. It works best with time series that have strong seasonal effects and several seasons of historical data. Prophet is robust to missing data and shifts in the trend, and typically handles outliers well." My LinkedIn Post About Facebook Prophet
